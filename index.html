Frequently asked FAQ

Quick answers about migrations, timelines & how I work.
Q1How long does a DanDomain to Shopify migration take?
Typically 1–6 weeks depending on catalog size, integrations and design complexity. A 1,000-product DanDomain shop usually ships in 2–3 weeks. I quote a fixed timeline up-front during planning.
Q2Will my SEO rankings survive a DanDomain to WordPress migration?
Yes — that's the entire point of doing it properly. URL-for-URL 301 redirect map, preserved title/meta/schema, ported sitemap and resubmitted to Google Search Console. Most projects hold or recover rankings within 30–60 days.
Q3Can you handle WordPress to Shopify migrations with custom plugins?
Yes. WooCommerce custom logic maps to Shopify equivalents — apps, metafields, Shopify Functions or custom theme code. Variable products convert to Shopify variants, blog posts move with redirects, design rebuilt on Online Store 2.0.
Q4How fast can you make my WordPress site?
Sub-1-second First Contentful Paint is the target on most WP sites. Proper cache stack — page cache, object cache (Redis), fragment cache, edge cache (Cloudflare) — plus OPcache tuning, critical CSS, lazy-load, and image CDN. Free speed audit included with any engagement.
Q5Do you keep DanDomain integrations like e-conomic or Business Central?
Yes. I re-establish the same integrations on the target platform: e-conomic, Business Central, Klaviyo, MailChimp, GLS / PostNord, payment gateways. Direct app where it exists, custom connector otherwise.
Q6What does a migration cost?
Fixed scope after the free discovery audit. Most DanDomain → Shopify migrations land between EUR 2,500 and EUR 12,000 depending on catalog, integrations and theme complexity. Quoted in writing up-front.
