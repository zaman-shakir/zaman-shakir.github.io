⌃ How it works

A migration that keeps
every ranking. Every URL. Every euro.

Three things have to happen on the same day or the cutover loses ranking authority:

  • 301 redirect map matched URL-for-URL from the old shop to the new one — typically 1,000–5,000 redirects, no orphans.
  • Structured data (Product / Offer / Review schema) matched to the old shop's schema so Google doesn't re-evaluate.
  • Crawl diff run by an independent tool within 48 hours of cutover — every priority URL signed off, in writing.

Most "lost ranking after migration" stories come from skipping one of these three. I've never skipped one. Zero failed cutovers in 50+ migrations.

⌃ Pre-call FAQ

Five things merchants ask before we talk.

If you have a different question, it'll get answered on the call. No tricks.

What happens on the discovery call?

30 minutes, screen-share. You walk me through your current DanDomain shop. I ask about catalog size, integrations, redirect surface, dual-pricing needs. By the end we both know whether the migration is a fit and what it would cost. No deck, no pitch.

Will I get a quote on the call?

You'll get a range — not a binding quote. Real quotes need 1–2 days of deeper inventory work and are issued in writing afterwards. No surprises. Fixed scope, fixed price, no hourly billing.

How do you keep SEO equity intact?

Three non-negotiables on cutover day: a complete 301 redirect map matched URL-for-URL, the new shop's structured data matched to the old schema, and an independent crawl diff within 48 hours. Most "lost ranking" stories skip at least one. I don't.

What integrations do you re-wire?

The Danish standard set — e-conomic, Business Central, Klaviyo, Judge.me, Mailchimp, Trustpilot, EAN/CVR billing flows, plus any custom ERPs or PIMs you depend on. Re-wired against the new Shopify schema during weeks 2–3.

What if my shop is too big / too small?

I take catalogs from 200 SKUs (small jewellery brand) up to 50,000 (multi-warehouse retailer). The shape of the migration changes; the workflow doesn't. The discovery call tells us which.
