The Migration — and what it cost to keep every ranking.

By Shakir Uz ZamanIndependent · Dhaka4 min read

Papirladen called me in late 2024. Their DanDomain shop was 12 years old, ran 38,000 SKUs across dual B2B and B2C catalogues, and ranked on the first page of Google for several thousand long-tail queries the wholesale industry depended on. The CEO had been told by three other agencies that a Shopify migration would cost €40,000+ and risk losing search authority for six months while Google "re-evaluated" the new site.

He didn't believe it. Neither did I.

Chapter IThe audit

Week one was the inventory. We walked every URL — 4,200 of them — and built the redirect map by hand. The B2B pricing logic, the EAN/CVR billing flow, the Klaviyo segments, the Judge.me reviews: each was diagrammed, dependencies labelled, replacements proposed on Shopify. By Friday of the first week we had a 23-page risk register. Both of us signed it. Nothing was built yet.

Chapter IIThe rebuild

Weeks two and three were the heads-down rebuild. A custom Liquid theme on staging. Catalog imported with metafields preserving every B2B price tier. Shopify Flow rewired to recreate the customer group logic that DanDomain handled natively. e-conomic integration plumbed through a small middleware layer. Klaviyo, Judge.me, Trustpilot: all reconnected against the new shop's webhooks. Every Friday I demoed live progress. Three change requests came back. All three landed inside the same week.

Chapter IIIThe cutover

Week four was the day everyone fears. We picked a Tuesday at 04:00 local time — the lowest-traffic window in the analytics. The 301 redirect map went live ten minutes before DNS flipped. Structured data was matched URL-for-URL to the old shop's schema so Google would never re-evaluate the page. By 04:14 the new shop was answering production traffic. I ran a crawl diff against the previous week's snapshot every two hours for the next 48. No URL dropped. No ranking moved. The first wholesale order came in at 09:22.

"He moved 38,000 SKUs and twelve years of SEO authority from DanDomain to Shopify in six weeks. Conversion went up the day we cut over."Jens Møller · Director · Papirladen.dk · est. 1987
Chapter IVThe numbers

Ninety days after cutover, the verdict was in. Conversion was up 38% on a like-for-like comparison. SEO equity was intact — actually slightly up, because Core Web Vitals had jumped from a B to an A. The B2B portal was being used by wholesalers who had previously called the office to place orders. The full project ran six weeks instead of the originally-scoped eight, and came in at the lower end of the fixed-price range.
