§ 01

The migration playbook — in plain English

No agency · no decks · only the work

The DanDomain platform served Danish merchants well for a decade. By 2026, the cracks have shown — theme rigidity, app ecosystem gaps, Core Web Vitals ceiling, B2B/B2C complexity. Merchants who depend on organic traffic and weekly conversion are quietly migrating to Shopify Plus.

The fear is always the same: "will I lose my rankings?" The answer depends on three things, all of which must happen on cutover day. A complete 301 redirect map, matched URL-for-URL. The new shop's structured data matched to the old schema. An independent crawl diff within 48 hours. Skip any one and rankings drop. Hit all three and traffic continues — or, in Papirladen's case, rises.

The cutover itself runs four phases over four to six weeks: discovery and audit (week 1), rebuild and rewire on staging (weeks 2–3), DNS flip during a low-traffic window (week 4), then stabilisation, crawl diff and hand-over (weeks 5–6). Fixed scope, fixed price, weekly Friday demos. When the project ends the merchant owns the code, the runbook and the keys.
