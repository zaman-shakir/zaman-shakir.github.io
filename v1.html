How a cutover runs four weeks, four phases

Fixed scope · weekly demos · single point of contact
Phase I · Week 1

Discovery & audit

Inventory of catalog, integrations, redirects, plugin debt. Cutover map drafted. Risk register signed off before work begins.

Phase II · Weeks 2–3

Rebuild & rewire

Theme rebuilt on staging. Catalog imported with metafields. Integrations (e-conomic, Business Central, Klaviyo, Judge.me) re-wired against the new schema.

Phase III · Week 4

Cutover

301 redirect map deployed. DNS flipped during low-traffic window. Live monitoring of every priority URL for 48 hours.

Phase IV · Post-launch

Stabilise

Two weeks of priority support included. SEO crawl diff, redirect coverage, cart funnel signal review. Then a handover doc, not a dependency.

§ 04

Frequent enquiries asked, answered

Updated quarterly
What does a DanDomain → Shopify migration cost?

Quoted per project after a paid discovery call. A typical catalog of 1–10k SKUs with two integrations and a redirect map under 5,000 URLs lands in the range of €8,000–€18,000 ex VAT. Larger B2B builds with Business Central or e-conomic wiring run higher. No hourly billing — fixed scope, fixed price.

How do you preserve SEO equity across a platform change?

Three things have to happen on the same day: a complete 301 redirect map matched URL-for-URL to the old site, the new shop's structured data matched to the old schema, and a crawl diff run by an independent tool within 48 hours of cutover. Most "lost ranking" stories come from skipping one of these. None of mine have.

Can you also run the WordPress build?

Yes. WordPress + WooCommerce is roughly a third of my practice. The same workflow applies — fixed scope, signed-off discovery, weekly demos, post-launch stabilisation. WP makes more sense than Shopify when content is the product, the merchant needs full data ownership, or the catalog model does not fit Shopify's variant ceiling.

How fast can a WP site actually load?

Most of my WP sites ship under 1.0s First Contentful Paint on a cold cache. That is a combination of page cache, object cache, edge fragment cache, OPcache, critical CSS inlining and a strict plugin diet. Speed is not magic — it is a budget, enforced.

Will I be a number on a roster?

No. The practice is independent and capacity is intentionally capped at two simultaneous migrations. You work with me directly, every week, with a single thread for questions and a Friday demo. When the project ends you own the code and the runbook.
